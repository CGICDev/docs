<Info>
  Prerequisites

  1. **Basic Setup**
     - Warehouses where you’ll store raw materials (e.g., “Raw Material Store”) are created under **Stock ▶️ Warehouse**.
     - Unit of Measure entries (e.g., Kilogram, Litre, Piece) exist under **Setup ▶️ UOM**.
  2. **Information on Hand**
     - **Material Name** (e.g., “Premium Wheat Flour”)
     - **Material Code** (unique, e.g. `RM-WF-25KG`)
     - **Default UOM** (e.g., Kilogram)
     - **Default Warehouse** (where stock is stored)
</Info>

### 1.0 Steps to Add a Raw Material

1. **Open the Item List**
   - From the home page, click **Stock ▶️ Item ▶️ Item List**.
2. **Create a New Item**
   - Click **New** in the top-right corner.
3. **Enter Basic Details**

| Field           | What to Enter                                    |
| --------------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| **Item Name**   | Premium Wheat Flour                              |
| **Item Code**   | RM-WF-25KG                                       |
| **Item Group**  | Select or create **Raw Materials**               |
| **Default UOM** | Kilogram                                         |
| **Description** | Brief notes, e.g., “High-grade flour for baking. |

4. **Inventory Settings**

Switch to the **Inventory** tab and fill in:

| Field                         | What to Enter & Why                                            |
| ----------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Shelf Life (Days)             | e.g. `180` – days before the material expires (for alerts)     |
| End of Life                   | e.g. `31-12-2029` – date when the material is no longer valid  |
| Default Material Request Type | `Purchase` – requests for this material default to Purchase    |
| Valuation Method              | `FIFO` or `Moving Average` – how stock cost is calculated      |
| Warranty Period (Days)        | if applicable) days covered under warranty after receipt       |
| Weight per Unit               | e.g. `25` – useful for shipping / production calculations      |
| Weight UOM                    | e.g. `Kilogram`                                                |
| Allow Negative Stock          | ✔️ (optional) – permits back-to-back selling when stock hits 0 |

5. **Define Barcodes (Optional)**

   Still on the **Inventory** tab, scroll to **Barcodes** and click **Add Row** to map any of these:

| Column           | Example & Purpose                            |
| ---------------- | -------------------------------------------- |
| **Barcode**      | `8901234567894` – the actual scanned barcode |
| **Barcode Type** | `EAN-13` – the barcode standard              |
| **UOM**          | `Each` – unit this barcode represents        |

6. **Purchasing Configuration**

- Click the **Purchasing** tab.
- **Purchase UOM**: confirm (e.g., “Kilogram”)
- **Supplier Details**:
  - Click **Add Row** under **Supplier Details**
  - **Supplier**: pick from your list (or create one if needed)
  - **Lead Time (Days)**: average delivery time
  - **Last Purchase Rate**: cost per UOM (for valuation)
  - ✔️ **Allow Purchase** should be checked

7. **Accounting Setup**
   - Click the **Accounting** tab.
   - **Valuation Method**: choose **FIFO** or **Moving Average**
   - **Expense Account**: e.g., “Cost of Goods Sold – Raw Materials”
   - **Stock Adjustment Account**: e.g., “Inventory Adjustments”
8. **Upload Image & Attachments** (optional)
   - Save the Item first.
   - Click the image placeholder to **Change** and upload a photo or spec sheet.
9. **Save Your New Raw Material**
   - Click **Save** in the top-right corner.
   - Your raw material is now available for purchase orders, stock entries, and BOMs.

<Tip>
  - **Shelf Life & End of Life**: Vital for perishable materials—set them to trigger expiry alerts.
  - **Valuation Method**: Choose according to your accounting policy; FIFO is common for raw ingredients.
  - **Weight Fields**: Power shipping and cost calculations automatically.
  - **Barcodes**: Streamline receiving and picking by scanning directly into transactions.
</Tip>
